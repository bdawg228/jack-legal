Privacy Policy

Jack · Last updated July 21, 2026

Jack (“the app”) is a private, internal tool operated by its owner (“we,” “us”) for the owner's own businesses. This policy explains what data the app handles and how. There are no other users: the app is not offered to the public and is not sold or listed.

What data the app accesses

With the owner's explicit authorization through Intuit's OAuth flow, the app reads the owner's own QuickBooks Online accounting data — such as company details, account balances, and financial reports (e.g. profit & loss, balance sheet). The app requests only the accounting scope (com.intuit.quickbooks.accounting).

Creating and updating records (with confirmation)

The app can create and update accounting records (for example invoices, customers, and transaction categorizations) — but only after the owner explicitly confirms each specific change. A built-in gate blocks every write until the owner approves that exact action; nothing is written automatically, in batches, or unattended. The following are hard-blocked and cannot be performed by the app under any circumstances: moving or committing money (payments, transfers, bill payments, deposits, refunds) and deleting records.

How data is stored

  • The app runs locally on the owner's own computer.
  • OAuth tokens (used to reconnect to QuickBooks) are stored locally on that computer with restricted file permissions.
  • Financial data is retrieved on demand to answer the owner's questions and is not warehoused in a database or retained beyond the session.

Who data is shared with

The app does not sell, rent, or share QuickBooks data with any third party, and there are no other users to share it with. To answer the owner's questions, the app may process retrieved data using its AI provider (Anthropic's Claude) under that provider's terms, solely to generate the owner's own results. No data is used for advertising, resale, disclosure to any other party, or to train AI models.

Data retention & deletion

The owner can revoke the app's access to QuickBooks at any time (see Disconnect), which invalidates the stored tokens. Deleting the local token files removes the app's ability to reconnect. Because data is not warehoused, there is no server-side store to purge.

Security

Access uses Intuit's standard OAuth 2.0. Tokens are kept on the owner's machine at restricted permissions and are not transmitted to any third-party server operated by us (we operate none).

Children's privacy

The app is a private business tool and is not directed to children.
